HOW TO COME

How to come to Norway?

Norway, nestled in the breathtaking landscapes of Scandinavia, is a country that captivates with its stunning natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. Renowned for its fjords, Norway boasts a coastline adorned with dramatic cliffs and serene, deep-blue inlets that carve through the rugged terrain.


Beyond its awe-inspiring landscapes, Norway embraces a deep cultural tapestry. The capital city, Oslo, is a vibrant hub of modernity and tradition, boasting world-class museums such as the Munch Museum and the Viking Ship Museum.


Norway's commitment to sustainability and environmental conservation is exemplary, with a focus on renewable energy and eco-friendly practices. The nation's social policies, including universal healthcare and free education, contribute to a high standard of living.

Main airports

Oslo

Oslo Airport, officially known as Oslo Gardermoen Airport, is the main international airport serving the capital city of Norway. Situated approximately 35 kilometers northeast of Oslo, it is the busiest airport in the country. The airport is characterized by modern facilities, efficient services, and a sleek Scandinavian design. With a wide range of amenities, including shops, restaurants, and convenient transportation links to the city center, Oslo Airport provides a gateway.

Bergen

Bergen Airport, officially Bergen Flesland Airport, is a major international airport located approximately 18 kilometers south of Bergen, Norway. Known for its picturesque surroundings, the airport is surrounded by mountains, offering travelers a scenic introduction to the region. With modern facilities, efficient services, and easy access to transportation options, provides a convenient entry point for visitors exploring the city and the stunning landscapes of western Norway.

Tromsø

Tromsø Airport, also known as Tromsø Langnes Airport, serves the city of Tromsø in northern Norway. Located just a few kilometers from the city center, the airport is a key transportation hub for the Arctic region. It features facilities, services, and offers travelers views of the surrounding Arctic landscapes upon arrival and departure. Tromsø Airport is well-equipped to handle both domestic and international flights, making it a gateway for those exploring the beauty of Norway.

Main ports

Ålesund

Ålesund's port is a picturesque maritime gateway situated on Norway's western coast. Framed by the town's distinctive art nouveau architecture, the port offers a charming blend of historical charm and coastal beauty. As a vital hub for fishing and trade, the port showcases a lively atmosphere with fishing boats, coastal vessels, and visiting cruise ships. With its scenic waterfront, views of the surrounding fjords, and proximity to Ålesund's cultural attractions, the port serves as a captivating entry point for enthusiasts and visitors exploring this gem.

Buy a ticket

Båtsfjord

Båtsfjord Port, located in the northeastern part of Norway, is a vital maritime hub in the spectacular Arctic region. Nestled on the Barents Sea coast, the port is known for its significance in the fishing industry, particularly for cod fishing. With a backdrop of rugged landscapes and Arctic waters, Båtsfjord Port provides a glimpse into the unique and challenging conditions of northern maritime activities. The port serves as a key point for fisheries-related operations and contributes to the economic vitality of the local community in this remote part of Norway.

Bergen

Bergen's port is a bustling maritime gateway on Norway's western coast, surrounded by the UNESCO-listed Bryggen Wharf. As a historic and thriving hub, the port welcomes cruise ships, cargo vessels, and local fishing boats. With a backdrop of colorful Hanseatic buildings and framed by majestic fjords, Bergen's port offers a captivating blend of maritime activity and scenic beauty, making it a must-visit destination for those exploring charm. The lively atmosphere of Bergen's port, set against a backdrop of mountains and deep blue waters.

Where can I stay?

Norwegian accommodations offer a diverse range of options, catering to various preferences and budgets. From charming cabins nestled in picturesque landscapes to modern hotels in bustling cities, Norway provides a unique blend of traditional and contemporary lodging experiences.


Visitors can immerse themselves in the country's natural beauty by staying in cabins or lodges, often located in scenic fjord-side or mountainous settings. Urban areas boast a selection of hotels that range from boutique to international chains, providing comfort and convenience.


Additionally, Norway's hospitality extends to unique options like ice hotels and historic guesthouses , offering travelers an array of choices to enhance their Norwegian experience.
