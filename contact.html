Accommodations tips

1. Appropriate Clothing: Norway has variable weather, even in summer. Make sure to bring waterproof and layered clothing to adapt to possible changes in weather.


2. Comfortable Footwear: If you plan to hike or explore nature, bring a good pair of sturdy and waterproof boots.


3. Power Adapter: Norway uses the European plug type with a voltage of 230V and a frequency of 50Hz. Make sure to bring an adapter if necessary.


4. Credit Card: Norway is a very modern country, and most places accept credit cards. Nevertheless, keep in mind that some remote places may have limited options , so carry some cash.


5. Explore Nature: Norway is famous for its stunning natural landscapes. Don't miss the opportunity to explore the fjords, glaciers, and national parks.


6. Respect for Nature: Norway takes pride in its natural environment, so make sure to adhere to outdoor conduct rules. Do not litter and respect protected areas.


7. Public Transportation: Public transportation in Norway is efficient and will take you to many popular destinations. Consider using trains and buses to get around.


8. Northern Lights: If you visit Norway in winter, especially in the north, you might have the chance to see the Northern Lights. Plan your visit based on weather conditions and the time of year.


9. Language: While many Norwegians speak English, learning some phrases in Norwegian can be appreciated. "Takk" means thank you, and "God dag" is a common greeting meaning good day.


10. Insurance: Make sure to have travel insurance that covers potential medical emergencies and trip cancellations.
